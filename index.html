1. IoT Device Testing & Debugging

2. Embedded System Validation

My role in embedded system validation encompassed end-to-end testing of microcontroller-based devices, ensuring compliance with industry standards and client-specific requirements. I conducted extensive validation of embedded systems utilizing communication protocols such as UART, I2C, and SPI, ensuring proper data transmission between hardware components. By implementing hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) testing methodologies, I was able to simulate real-world scenarios and validate system performance under various environmental conditions.

One of my key responsibilities was conducting power efficiency tests to optimize energy consumption for battery-powered IoT devices. I also worked on debugging embedded firmware issues by using serial communication logs and debugging tools. Collaborating with cross-functional teams, I played a crucial role in refining software-hardware interactions, ensuring that embedded systems met design specifications and operated reliably in production environments.

3. Network Connectivity Testing

As part of my IoT engineering responsibilities, I specialized in testing network connectivity for IoT-enabled devices. I validated connectivity reliability in challenging conditions such as low-signal environments, high-latency networks, and congested bandwidth scenarios. My role involved testing the robustness of wireless communication protocols including Wi-Fi, LoRaWAN, and Bluetooth, ensuring uninterrupted data transmission and minimal packet loss.

I developed simulation environments that replicated real-world connectivity issues to measure the resilience of IoT networks under stress conditions. Through network diagnostics and performance analysis, I optimized communication efficiency by fine-tuning protocol configurations. Additionally, I worked on troubleshooting network failures, implementing redundancy mechanisms, and ensuring IoT devices remained connected and functional in dynamic network environments.
